Combustion Chamber

Extends : MetroscopeModelingLibrary.Common.Partial.BasicTransportModel

TSP handbook : Chapter 8

Combustion is a chemical reaction that occurs between a fuel and an oxidizing agent that produces energy, usually in the form of heat and light. During combustion, oxygen is combined with different elements of the fuel, like carbon, hydrogen, sulfur, etc. and produces the respective oxides. Some excess air is always supplied to the burner, to ensure complete combustion. (TSP Handbook)

The MML combustion chamber extends a Basic Transport Model. It has an inlet and outlet for air, and an additionnal inlet of fuel (black inlet).

⚠️ The fuel connector is not part of the basic transport model, so its physical quantities have to be accessed using the connector name C_fuel.

⚠️ For this combustion chamber, the LHV is a parameter. If the LHV needs to be determined using the enthalpy of formation of the different species, use the CombustionChamber_LHV.

See eq. 3 page 169. The combustion is supposed to be complete, which means the combustion efficiency as defined in the TSP handbook is set to 1. There is no air atomization nor thermal power losses.

H_fuelconnector designates the fuel hydraulic power at the inlet C_fuel.H. It cannot be computed as Qfuel*hfuel since hfuel is not defined, due to the fact that this connector is not integrated in the basic transport model. In order to complete the system, the h_vol of the fuel connector also has to be specified (to an arbitrary value).
