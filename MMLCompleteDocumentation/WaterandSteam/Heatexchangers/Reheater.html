Reheater

The reheater is a shell and tube heat exchanger. The goal is to reheat the water in the cold leg using steam that is condensated in the reheater. The heat exchange is separated in 3 parts :

  • The deheating phase in which the steam temperature is reduced.
  • The condensing phase in which it changes phase.
  • The purge or subcool phase in which it is further cooled.

For the sake of simplicity it is assumed that all pressure loss occur during the first phase. The level parameter describes the level of the liquid phase on the hot side.

The component has 4 connectors : two connectors for the hot fluid (inlet at the top for steam, outlet at the bottom) and two connectors for the cold fluit (on the sides). Inside, it is made of two lines of BasicTransportModels that exchange heat, and represent the three phases of the heat exchange. Due to the separation in different parts, the direction is important, and the component is not symetric. Be careful that is it is in the right direction in your model.

Deheating

It is assumed that there is first pressure loss and then heat exchange. The deheating then depends of the enthalpy at the inlet. If it is above the enthalpy of the dew at the saturation pressure, then there is deheating. Otherwise, the condensation phase starts immediately, and the the deheating surface and heat transfer are set to zero.

In case of deheating, the heat exchange WdehW_{deh} Wdeh​﻿ is computed using the heat exchange MML function. See Common.Functions. The HotPhase parameter is set to 1 since the water in both sides is liquid. The exchange coefficient is hdehh_{deh}hdeh​﻿ and the exchange area is AdehA_{deh}Adeh​﻿. At the end of the deheating phase, the enthalpy of the hot fluid is equal to the enthalpy of the dew at the saturation pressure, meaning that the steam is ready to condensate.

Condensation

Following the enthalpy at the inlet of the condensation part on the hot side, there will be condensation or not. If the enthalpy is under the liquid (bubble) enthalpy at the saturation pressure, then there is no condensation. The outlet enthalpy for the condensation part of the hot leg is set to this value, and the exchange surface and heat transfer for condensation are set to zero.

If there is condensation, the heat exchange is computed using the heat transfer function. The hot phase parameter is set to 0.5, since the water is biphasic on the hot side. The exchange coefficient is hcondh_{cond}hcond​﻿ and the exchange area is AcondA_{cond}Acond​﻿. At the end of the condensation section, the water is completely liquid, hence the enthalpy is equal to the bubble enthalpy.

The energy balance for this part of the transfer is :

Subcooled zone (purge)

In the subcool zone, there is a heat transfer between two liquids. The heat exchange is determined using the heat transfer function.
