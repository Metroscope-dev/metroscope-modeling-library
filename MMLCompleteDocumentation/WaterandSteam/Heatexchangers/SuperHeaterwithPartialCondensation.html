SuperHeater with Partial Condensation

The superheater is a shell and tube heat exchanger. The goal is to superheat the steam that has beenthrough the high pressure turbine using steam directly from the steam generator. The steam on the out side, usually coming from the steam generators, is partly condensed in the process.

The cold side is the side with the steam that will be superheated. First it is assumed that there is some liquid remaining in the steam, therefore there is a first phase in which the cold fluid is vaporized. It is then superheated.

The hot side is the side with the hot steam that will be condensed. First it is desuperheated since it is assumed that its pressure may have changed before arriving to the superheater. It is then partly condensed.

In order to impose that the hot steam will flow correctly, a ventilation is imposed. For this model, this is replaced by an extraction of hot steam equivalent to the ventilation, and that is about 1% of the total steam.

The architecture of the superheater is given in the figure below. The combination of the different phases of both sides gives 3 zones :

  • A Deheating / Superheating part in which the hot steam is deheated and the cold steam superheated. This is the DesHSupH part. This part doesn't exist if the intlet hot steam is at saturation.
  • A Condensing / Vaporizing part in which the cold steam is vaporized and the hot steam is condensed. This is the CondVap part. This part doesn't exist if the inlet cold steam has a steam fraction of 1.
  • A main part in which the hot steam is condensed and the cold steam is superheated. This is the CondSupH part, and the function of the superheater.

For each part, there is a basic transport model for each side, and an associated heat exchange W. There is an additionnal basic transport model for the ventilation.

The cold side has two connectors : one inlet and one outlet. The hot side has 3 connectors : one inlet, one outlet and one ventilation outlet.

For simplicity, the pressure loss on both side are supposed to happen only in the first part of each side. At the end of the hot side, the steam is completely condensed.

Heat exchange during the CondVap part

The heat exchange during the CondVap part is determined by the state of the cold steam at the inlet. If its enthalpy is over the saturation enthalpy of the steam, then there is no vaporization part, therefore WCondVap=0W_{CondVap} = 0WCondVap​=0﻿.

If there is vaporization, then it is total, and therefore the enthalpy of the cold steam at the end of this phase is determined :

hout,cold,CondVap=hv,coldsath_{out,cold,CondVap} = h_{v,cold}^{sat}hout,cold,CondVap​=hv,coldsat​﻿

The heat exchange is then determined by the energy balance equation of the cold side for this part.

In both cases, it is the end of the condensation for the hot side, therefore :

hout,hot,CondVap=(1−xout)hl,hotsat+xouthv,hsath_{out,hot,CondVap} = (1-x_{out})h_{l,hot}^{sat} + x_{out}h_{v,h}^{sat}hout,hot,CondVap​=(1−xout​)hl,hotsat​+xout​hv,hsat​﻿

Heat exchange during the CondSupH part

This is the normal function of the heat exchanger. In this part, the heat transfer is a classic shell and tubes counter-flow heat exchanger with a vapor-liquid phase on the hot side. The heat transfer is therefore determined by the HeatExchange function with the phase parameter set to 0.5.

Heat exchange during the DesHSupH part

The heat exchange during the CondVap part is determined by the state of the hot steam at the inlet. If this steam is superheated, for instance due to pressure losses, then it has to be deheated before it is condensed.
