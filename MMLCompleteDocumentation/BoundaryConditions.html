Boundary Conditions

Partial Boundary Condition

Inherits from : Common.Constants.Constants

The Partial Boundary Condition is the basis of the sources and sinks. It cannot be used on its own. It has only one connecter C. It defines the following quantities :

  • Pressure P
  • Mass flow rate Q
  • Enthalpy h
  • Mass Fractions X_i
  • Temperature T and thermodynamic state state.

The power H at the connector is then Q*h, and the mass flow rate of species i at the connector is Q*X_i.

It also defines "volumic" quantites. These quantities will be used only if the Boundary Condition acts as a source, or reservoir, hence the word "volumic". It is then assumed that the Boundary Condition is an infinite tank of enthalphy h_vol, temperature T_vol, mass fracions X_vol_i, and thermodynamic state state_vol.

Source

A source is made of a partial boundary condition, a basic transport model, and an out connector.

As the partial boundary condition does not deal with the inversion of flow rate and h_vol, a basic transport model is needed to connect the partial boundary condition to the rest of the circuit.

There is no pressure loss or energy loss in the basic transport model.

Sink

A sink is made of a partial boundary condition, a basic transport model, and an in connector.

As the partial boundary condition does not deal with the inversion of flow rate and h_vol, a basic transport model is needed to connect the partial boundary condition to the rest of the circuit.

There is no pressure loss or energy loss in the basic transport model.

Loop Closer

Extends : MetroscopeModelingLibrary.Common.Partial.BasicTransportModel

The loop closer is used to avoid an overconstrained system when closing a loop. It has no physical meaning, it is simply a computation artefact. Let's say there is loop with two components A and B. The mass conservation for component A will be QA=QB and for component B QB=QA, therefore the system has the same equation twice and is singular.

In the loop closer, only the enthalpy and pressure are propagated, so that the flow is free. The system of equations is then QA=Q_loopcloser at component A and QB=Q_loopcloser at component B. In the end, it is equivalent to only one equation QA=QB for the whole system.
