Pressure Loss

The pressure loss components model various pressure losses in the system, either singular pressure loss (like a change of geometry of the pipe), pipe pressure loss due to friction or height, valves... They all inherit from the Partial Pressure Loss component.

Pressure loss modeling is chapter 13 of the TSP handbook.

Partial Pressure Loss

Inherits from : BasicTransportModel

A partial pressure loss is a simple basic transport model in which the flow rate is equal at the outlet and outlet :

Qin+Qout=0Q_{in} + Q_{out} = 0Qin​+Qout​=0﻿

The pressure loss is defined as the difference between the outlet pressure and the inlet pressure :

ΔP=Pout−Pin\Delta P = P_{out} - P_{in}ΔP=Pout​−Pin​﻿

Singular Pressure Loss

Inherits from : Partial Pressure Loss

The singular pressure loss is just a simple partial pressure loss with a specific icon. Its only parameter is therefore ΔP\Delta PΔP﻿.

There is not heat loss in this component, therefore :

Qin∗hin+Qout∗hout=0Q_{in}*h_{in} + Q_{out}*h_{out} = 0Qin​∗hin​+Qout​∗hout​=0﻿
